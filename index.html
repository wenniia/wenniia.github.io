Annie Wang is a self-taught digital artist studying electrical engineering at the University of California, Santa Barbara. With a profound interest in expressing creations reflecting the intracacies of light, she examines how the properties of light distort and carve form, altering how people understand and verify their perceptions of the real world.

Light speed

is a high-speed reaction game where the player must hit eight illuminated buttons as fast as possible within the alloted time frame. This Batak-inspired rendition capitalizes on the original board's interactive nature and friendly competition while boosting ease of portability and vivid colors.

Magic Mirror

is a mirror that displays and self-updates with pockets of information. Taken from the user, relevant information such as daily tasks, events, and weather is able to be consolidated all onto one screen. Its concept originates from the ability to start the day off able to process a day's responsibilities at a glance.

Arduino Pong Game

is a rendition of the classic 1972 arcade game. Displayed on the colorful SSD1331, this miniature game features both single and multiplayer modes.

AIRA

is the Artificial Intelligence Raccoon Automaton, an Arduino and LLM-powered robot that is able to interact with its audience through sound, specifically human speech.
